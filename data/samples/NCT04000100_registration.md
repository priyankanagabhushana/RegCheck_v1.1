# Registration: Randomized Controlled Trial of Cognitive Behavioral Therapy for Generalized Anxiety Disorder

## ClinicalTrials.gov ID: NCT04000100

## Brief Summary
This trial evaluates the efficacy of 12 weeks of cognitive behavioral therapy (CBT) compared to waitlist control for adults with generalized anxiety disorder (GAD). The primary hypothesis is that CBT will reduce anxiety symptoms by at least 30% compared to waitlist.

## Study Design
- Type: Interventional (Randomized)
- Allocation: Randomized
- Masking: Single blind (outcomes assessor)
- Primary purpose: Treatment

## Primary Outcome
- Measure: GAD-7 Anxiety Scale
- Time frame: 12 weeks post-randomization
- Description: 7-item self-report measure of generalized anxiety disorder symptoms

## Secondary Outcomes
- Measure: PHQ-9 Depression Scale
  Time frame: 12 weeks post-randomization
- Measure: Pittsburgh Sleep Quality Index (PSQI)
  Time frame: 12 weeks post-randomization

## Sample Size
- Planned enrollment: 200 participants
- Power analysis: Power=0.80, alpha=0.05, effect size d=0.50, two-tailed
- Expected dropout: 15%

## Eligibility Criteria
### Inclusion
- Age 18-65 years
- Meet DSM-5 criteria for GAD
- GAD-7 score ≥ 10
- Able to provide informed consent

### Exclusion
- Current suicidal ideation (PHQ-9 item 9 ≥ 2)
- Concurrent psychotherapy
- Changes in psychotropic medication within past 4 weeks
- Primary diagnosis of substance use disorder
- Bipolar disorder or psychotic disorder

## Analysis Plan
Primary analysis: ANCOVA with post-treatment GAD-7 as dependent variable, controlling for baseline GAD-7, age, gender, and medication status. Multiple comparison correction: Bonferroni for secondary outcomes. Software: R 4.3.0.
